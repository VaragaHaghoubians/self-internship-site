Supply Chain AI Domain (Your Target Industry)

The three technical pillars that make you an Operations Data Scientist. Study these alongside and after Phase 2.

Pillar 1 — Demand Forecasting (Predicting the Future)

ResourceURLWhen to use it
Store Item Demand Forecasting Challenge (Kaggle)https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/demand-forecasting-kernels-onlyMonth 2–3 — classic dataset: predict 3 months of item sales across store locations. Study top notebooks first.
DataCo Smart Supply Chain Dataset (Kaggle)https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/shashwatwork/dataco-smart-supply-chain-for-big-data-analysisMonth 5+ — massive real-world dataset covering provisioning, delivery, pricing, and routing. Perfect for Phase 3 projects.
Olist Brazilian E-Commerce Dataset (Kaggle)https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/olistbr/brazilian-ecommerceMonth 2 or 5 — 100k real orders. Practice delivery delay analysis, logistics performance, and customer satisfaction.

Pillar 2 — Inventory Optimization (Balancing the Scale)

ResourceURLWhen to use it
Search: "Inventory Management using Python"https://towardsdatascience.com/Month 3–4 — search this site for EOQ, safety stock, and Monte Carlo simulation tutorials
Key skills: EOQ (Economic Order Quantity) as a Python function, Monte Carlo simulation with numpy/scipy to find optimal safety stock levels, stochastic demand modeling.

Pillar 3 — Network & Route Optimization (Moving the Goods)

Already covered in Superpower 1 above. OR-Tools VRP is the primary tool.
Additional library: NetworkX — maps warehouse locations as graph nodes and finds shortest paths.
ResourceURLWhen to use it
NetworkX Documentationhttps://networkx.org/documentation/stable/Month 9 — model your supply chain network as a directed graph before running the optimizer

Management Information Systems (MIS) — The Enterprise Layer

The digital nervous system of a company. Where your Engineering Management degree meets your coding skills.

Pillar 1 — Data Architecture & ETL

ResourceURLWhen to use it
Kimball Group — Dimensional Modelling (Star Schema)https://www.kimballgroup.com/data-warehouse-business-intelligence-resources/kimball-techniques/dimensional-modeling-techniques/Before Month 2 SQL work — the industry standard for designing databases that managers can query
Key skills: Star Schema vs. Snowflake Schema design, fact tables vs. dimension tables, ETL pipelines in Python (pandas + SQLAlchemy), automating with Celery (Month 6).

Pillar 2 — Business Intelligence & Dashboards

ResourceURLWhen to use it
Metabase (open-source BI)https://www.metabase.com/docs/latest/Month 3 — spin up a Metabase container with Docker and connect it to your SQL database for enterprise-grade dashboards in minutes
Apache Supersethttps://superset.apache.org/Month 3–5 — alternative to Metabase; heavier but more powerful for custom SQL charts
Plotly Dash Tutorialshttps://dash.plotly.com/tutorialMonth 5–6 — embed interactive BI dashboards directly inside your Django app

Pillar 3 — Enterprise Automation & Workflow Orchestration

ResourceURLWhen to use it
Apache Airflow Documentationhttps://airflow.apache.org/docs/Month 4–5 — industry standard for orchestrating ETL pipelines and scheduled ML jobs
n8n (no-code/low-code automation)https://n8n.io/Month 5 — visually wire together APIs, databases, and alerts (Slack, email) for internal business workflows

Related workspace files:
