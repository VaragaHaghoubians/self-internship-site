Statistics (Basic)

You do not need advanced stats. You need these three things:

  1. Chi-square test — tests if two categorical variables are independent.
  • Example: "Is Contract type independent of Churn, or are they related?"
  • Library: scipy.stats.chi2_contingency
  • Read: what a p-value means. If p < 0.05, the relationship is statistically significant.
  1. What "class imbalance" means — when one class (e.g., "No churn") appears far more often than the other ("Churn"). This skews model accuracy. Know how to handle it.
  1. Correlation — df.corr() gives the Pearson correlation matrix. Values near 1 or -1 mean strong linear relationship.

Evaluation — do NOT just use accuracy

MetricWhat it measuresWhy it matters
PrecisionOf all predicted churners, how many actually churned?Avoid wasting retention offers on loyal customers
RecallOf all actual churners, how many did we catch?Missing a churner is expensive — recall must be high
F1-scoreHarmonic mean of precision and recallThe single metric that balances both
ROC-AUCModel's ability to distinguish classes at all thresholdsStandard business reporting metric
Confusion matrixRaw TP, TN, FP, FN countsShows exactly what type of mistakes the model makes

Business Concepts

You will be asked these in every interview. Know the answers before you submit the project.

QuestionAnswer to memorise
Why predict churn instead of just tracking it?Prediction enables intervention before the customer leaves. Tracking only tells you what already happened.
Why does class imbalance matter here?Most customers don't churn. A model that predicts "No churn" for everyone would have 85% accuracy but be completely useless.
Which metric matters more — precision or recall?Recall. Missing a churner costs 5–7x more than falsely flagging a loyal customer for a retention call.
What is a "feature importance"?How much each input variable contributes to the model's predictions. RandomForestClassifier.feature_importances_ gives this.
